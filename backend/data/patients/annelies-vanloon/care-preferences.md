# Care preferences — Sister Annelies van Loon

## Bathing and personal care
- Female caregivers only for any personal care. This is a vow-of-modesty matter, not a preference — she has never consented otherwise in 84 years and will not start now. {confidence: high, sources: [n_001#c_7], first_seen: 2026-04-01, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- She prefers to wash her own face and hands at the basin first; then accepts help with the rest. Skipping the self-wash step makes her irritable. {confidence: high, sources: [n_004#c_6], first_seen: 2026-04-12, last_confirmed: 2026-04-23}
- **Hum a Swahili hymn during bathing — "Tukutendereza Yesu" works best.** She joins in quietly and her shoulders drop. {confidence: high, sources: [n_003#c_6, n_006#c_2], first_seen: 2026-04-08, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- Always offer the headscarf before stepping out of the bathroom — she does not consider herself dressed without it. {confidence: high, sources: [n_001#c_8], first_seen: 2026-04-01, last_confirmed: 2026-04-23}
- Cool water on the face, never warm. ("Warm water voelt als koorts." — "warm water feels like a fever.") {confidence: medium, sources: [n_004#c_7], first_seen: 2026-04-12, last_confirmed: 2026-04-22}
- **Do not touch the rosary on the bedside.** It belonged to her mother (1903 enamel). It can be moved aside with the back of a cloth if absolutely necessary; otherwise, work around it. {confidence: high, sources: [n_002#c_8, n_005#c_4], first_seen: 2026-04-03, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}

## Eating and drinking
- **10:30 every morning: strong black coffee with one square of dark chocolate.** Non-negotiable. She will get up and walk to the kitchen herself if it doesn't appear. {confidence: high, sources: [n_001#c_9, n_004#c_8], first_seen: 2026-04-01, last_confirmed: 2026-04-25}
- Breakfast is bread with jam and a cup of tea, after Lauds. She is not hungry before prayer. {confidence: high, sources: [n_001#c_10], first_seen: 2026-04-01, last_confirmed: 2026-04-23}
- **Likes spicy food — pili-pili, ginger, anything with bite.** A surprise to most carers. Bland Dutch hospital food makes her sad. Keep a bottle of hot sauce in her cupboard. {confidence: high, sources: [n_003#c_7, n_006#c_3], first_seen: 2026-04-08, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- Eats meat sparingly; no pork (order tradition, not strict rule, but she keeps it). Fish on Fridays. {confidence: medium, sources: [n_002#c_9], first_seen: 2026-04-03, last_confirmed: 2026-04-22}
- Will not start any meal without grace. Even a cup of coffee gets a small sign of the cross. Wait for it. {confidence: high, sources: [n_001#c_11], first_seen: 2026-04-01, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- Glass of water by the bed at night, room temperature. Cold water gives her a cough. {confidence: medium, sources: [n_004#c_9], first_seen: 2026-04-12}

## Morning routine
- **06:15 wake; 06:30 Lauds at the chapel downstairs (or in her room with the small breviary if unwell).** Do not wake her later "to let her sleep". She will be more distressed by missing prayer than by getting up. {confidence: high, sources: [n_001#c_6, n_004#c_5], first_seen: 2026-04-01, last_confirmed: 2026-04-25}
- After prayer, breakfast, then she sits with the BBC World Service on the radio (Africa news, low). {confidence: high, sources: [n_004#c_10], first_seen: 2026-04-12, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- Medication is taken with the morning coffee at 10:30, not breakfast. She has fasted before Lauds her whole life and will not break that for pills. {confidence: high, sources: [n_002#c_10], first_seen: 2026-04-03, last_confirmed: 2026-04-23}

## Daily rhythms (the unwritten ones)
- **Waters and talks to her succulents on the windowsill every afternoon.** Each one is named after a saint (Sint Theresia, Sint Franciscus, Sint Kizito, etc.). She will introduce them. Play along — these are her companions. {confidence: high, sources: [n_005#c_5, n_006#c_4], first_seen: 2026-04-15, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- **BBC Africa news at 17:00, volume too loud.** Do not turn it down without warning. If it bothers neighbours, ask her to lower it — she will. {confidence: high, sources: [n_006#c_5], first_seen: 2026-04-18, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- Vespers at 18:00, then a light supper, then she writes letters by hand to former pupils until about 20:30. Many letters never get sent — that's fine, the writing is the point. {confidence: medium, sources: [n_005#c_6], first_seen: 2026-04-15, last_confirmed: 2026-04-23}
- Lights out around 21:30 with a short prayer in French. {confidence: medium, sources: [n_004#c_11], first_seen: 2026-04-12, last_confirmed: 2026-04-22}

## Things she has explicitly asked us not to do
- **Do not mention Rwanda 1994, the genocide, or "what happened over there".** Not in any context. Not even kindly. {confidence: high, sources: [n_003#c_5, n_005#c_1], first_seen: 2026-04-08, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- Do not move the small wooden cross from under her pillow. She checks for it nightly. (Carved in Bukavu, 1971.) {confidence: high, sources: [n_002#c_11, n_005#c_7], first_seen: 2026-04-03, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- Do not rearrange or repot the succulents. The order on the windowsill is a calendar of saints' feast days, in her head. {confidence: medium, sources: [n_005#c_8], first_seen: 2026-04-15, last_confirmed: 2026-04-23, attributed: family/Sister Magda}
- Do not call her "Annelieske". See `personhood.md`. {confidence: high, sources: [n_002#c_6], first_seen: 2026-04-03, last_confirmed: 2026-04-22}
