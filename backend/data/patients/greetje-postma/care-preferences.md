# Care preferences — Greetje Postma

## Bathing and personal care
- Washing at the basin, seated, in the bedroom. **Not the bathroom — the tile floor disorients her.** Tjitske brings a bowl of warm water to the chair. {confidence: high, sources: [n_002#c_6, n_006#c_7], first_seen: 2026-04-05, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- **Always wash the right (good) side first, then the left. The weak side last and gentlest.** Starting on the left makes her flinch and pull away. {confidence: high, sources: [n_002#c_7], first_seen: 2026-04-05, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- Hum a Frisian psalm — "Wat de takomst bringe mij" works — at low volume during washing. She slows her breathing within seconds. {confidence: high, sources: [n_004#c_4, n_006#c_8], first_seen: 2026-04-15, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- Female caregivers only for intimate care. She has been firm on this since the stroke. {confidence: high, sources: [n_001#c_5], first_seen: 2026-04-01, last_confirmed: 2026-04-23}
- **No perfumes, no scented soap.** Plain green soap (Sunlight) only. Anything floral makes her say "rûkt as in begraffenis" — smells like a funeral. {confidence: high, sources: [n_006#c_9, n_008#c_3], first_seen: 2026-04-12, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}

## Eating and drinking
- **Karnemelk (buttermilk) with breakfast, every day. A full glass.** This is non-negotiable; she has drunk it her entire life. {confidence: high, sources: [n_001#c_6, n_006#c_10], first_seen: 2026-04-01, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- **Coffee is for guests, not for her.** Do not offer coffee in the morning. Tea is acceptable, water is fine, karnemelk is right. {confidence: high, sources: [n_001#c_7], first_seen: 2026-04-01, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- Brown bread with cheese (jong belegen, sliced thin) for breakfast and often lunch. **Never sweet things in the morning** — no jam, no hagelslag, no sugar. "Snobbersguod is foar bern" (sweets are for children). {confidence: high, sources: [n_006#c_11], first_seen: 2026-04-12, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- **Will not eat off plates with patterns.** "Voor het oog te druk." Plain white or cream only. Tjitske keeps a stack in the lower-left cupboard. {confidence: high, sources: [n_006#c_12, n_008#c_4], first_seen: 2026-04-12, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- Hot meal at midday, always. Potatoes, vegetable, a bit of meat or fish. The evening meal is bread again. Reverse this and she will not eat. {confidence: high, sources: [n_002#c_8], first_seen: 2026-04-05, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- Drinks small sips of water through the day from a glass on the kitchen sill — not a "drinking aid" beaker. The beaker offends her. {confidence: medium, sources: [n_004#c_5], first_seen: 2026-04-15, last_confirmed: 2026-04-23}

## Morning routine
- **Up at 06:30, restless if kept in bed past 07:00.** "De kij wachtsje net" — "the cows don't wait" — even though there are no cows now. Get her up. {confidence: high, sources: [n_001#c_8, n_006#c_13], first_seen: 2026-04-01, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- First action: she touches the kitchen bell on the wall, looks out the window at the fields, then sits. **Do not rush this sequence.** {confidence: high, sources: [n_006#c_3, n_009#c_1], first_seen: 2026-04-12, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- Karnemelk, brown bread, cheese, in that order, at the kitchen table by the window. Radio Fryslân on, low. {confidence: high, sources: [n_001#c_6], first_seen: 2026-04-01, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- Medication after the bread, with a sip of water. Not before; she gags on an empty stomach. {confidence: medium, sources: [n_002#c_9], first_seen: 2026-04-05, last_confirmed: 2026-04-22}

## Daily rhythms (the unwritten ones)
- **Folds tea towels and napkins at the kitchen table mid-morning.** Tjitske rotates a stack so there are always some to fold. This is work; she needs it. {confidence: high, sources: [n_002#c_5, n_008#c_5], first_seen: 2026-04-05, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
- **Afternoon nap in the chair by the window — never the bed.** The bed is for night. {confidence: high, sources: [n_004#c_6], first_seen: 2026-04-15, last_confirmed: 2026-04-24}
