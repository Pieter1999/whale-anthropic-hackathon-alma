# Care preferences — Theodora "Tante Toos" Pengel

## Bathing and personal care
- Warm-to-hot water, never lukewarm. She grew up bathing in heat and finds tepid water unpleasant ("kouwe boel"). {confidence: high, sources: [n_002#c_5, n_006#c_10], first_seen: 2026-04-05, last_confirmed: 2026-04-23}
- **Coconut oil for her skin after bathing — her own bottle, on the bathroom shelf.** Not the lotion the home-care service brings. She will refuse the lotion. {confidence: high, sources: [n_004#c_4, n_006#c_11], first_seen: 2026-04-15, last_confirmed: 2026-04-25}
- Female caregivers preferred for intimate care. She has tolerated a male caregiver once, in a pinch, but it was not a happy day. Avoid. {confidence: high, sources: [n_001#c_5, n_003#c_4], first_seen: 2026-04-01, last_confirmed: 2026-04-22}
- Hum or sing while bathing — she relaxes. Soraya hums "Faja siton" softly during washes; Tante sometimes joins on the chorus. {confidence: high, sources: [n_002#c_6, n_006#c_12], first_seen: 2026-04-05, last_confirmed: 2026-04-25, attributed: family/Soraya}
- Headscarf on Sundays only — a thin yellow or orange one, folded in the top dresser drawer. Do not put one on her on a weekday; she will take it off and frown. {confidence: high, sources: [n_006#c_13, n_008#c_4], first_seen: 2026-04-12, last_confirmed: 2026-04-25}
- Her gold earrings stay in. She has worn them since 1962. They come out only at the hospital, by her, never by staff. {confidence: high, sources: [n_007#c_7], first_seen: 2026-04-15, last_confirmed: 2026-04-23}

## Eating and drinking
- **Strong, very sweet ginger tea (gemberthee)** — two thick slices of fresh ginger, a generous spoon of cane sugar, boiled five minutes. Coffee never; she calls coffee "bitter water". {confidence: high, sources: [n_001#c_6, n_006#c_14], first_seen: 2026-04-01, last_confirmed: 2026-04-25}
- **Refuses to use the microwave — "die ding maakt het eten dood".** Reheat on the stove or not at all. This is non-negotiable. {confidence: high, sources: [n_002#c_7, n_006#c_15], first_seen: 2026-04-05, last_confirmed: 2026-04-25}
- Surinamese food is the default. Pom, bruine bonen met rijst, moksi alesi, roti on Wednesdays. She will eat Dutch food but doesn't enjoy it; "eten zonder smaak". {confidence: high, sources: [n_007#c_8, n_009#c_3], first_seen: 2026-04-15, last_confirmed: 2026-04-25, attributed: family/Soraya}
- **Pom is her dish of authority.** When pom is being made, she runs the kitchen. Let her. Even if she repeats the same instruction four times, follow it. {confidence: high, sources: [n_006#c_16, n_008#c_5], first_seen: 2026-04-12, last_confirmed: 2026-04-25}
- Eats with a spoon, not a fork, for most meals — including rice. This is not regression, this is preference. Fork on the table is fine, just don't insist. {confidence: medium, sources: [n_004#c_5], first_seen: 2026-04-15, last_confirmed: 2026-04-22}
- A peppermint after dinner. Always. The tin is in her dressing-gown pocket. {confidence: medium, sources: [n_007#c_9], first_seen: 2026-04-15, last_confirmed: 2026-04-23}

## Morning routine
- Wakes around 06:00, before the household. Sits at the kitchen table with the radio (RBN, Surinamese station) on low, waiting for the kettle. {confidence: high, sources: [n_002#c_8, n_006#c_17], first_seen: 2026-04-05, last_confirmed: 2026-04-25}
- Ginger tea before anything, while she watches the gallery wake up. {confidence: high, sources: [n_002#c_9], first_seen: 2026-04-05, last_confirmed: 2026-04-23}
- Breakfast small: a slice of bread with cheese, or a banana. She doesn't want a full breakfast; the kitchen day is about lunch and dinner. {confidence: medium, sources: [n_004#c_6], first_seen: 2026-04-15, last_confirmed: 2026-04-22}
- Medication with breakfast, never on an empty stomach. She has refused pills before tea, every time. {confidence: high, sources: [n_002#c_10], first_seen: 2026-04-05, last_confirmed: 2026-04-22}

## Daily rhythms (the unwritten ones)
- **"Helping" in the kitchen** — late morning, every day. Soraya sets out a bowl of onions to peel, garlic to crush, or a pot to stir. Tante does the task, sometimes twice. Do not "spare" her this — it is her grounding ritual, not a chore. {confidence: high, sources: [n_002#c_4, n_008#c_3, n_009#c_4], first_seen: 2026-04-05, last_confirmed: 2026-04-25, attributed: family/Soraya}

- Bedtime around 21:00 with the bible touched but not read. {confidence: medium, sources: [n_003#c_3], first_seen: 2026-04-08, last_confirmed: 2026-04-22}
