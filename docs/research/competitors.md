# Competitor Landscape — Voice-Backed Care Passport

Last checked: 2026-04-25

Scope: who else is going after "capture the patient's voice early so professionals can deliver more personal dementia care later" — and how we should position. Skips obvious non-fits; flags overlap, defensibility, and gaps.

## A. Voice-capture / life-story / digital-legacy products

### Memoirji — https://memoirji.com/
- **What:** WhatsApp-based AI biographer ("Patience") that interviews users via voice notes and produces a structured PDF memoir, free.
- **Geography / stage:** Indie consumer, global. Positioned in 2026 round-ups as the leading WhatsApp-native life-story tool ([Memoirji blog](https://memoirji.com/blog/best-ai-memoir-tools-2025/)).
- **Overlap (axis 1):** High on input channel — same WhatsApp + voice-note wedge we plan to use for capture. They explicitly target elderly parents.
- **Why we still win:** Memoirji's output is a memoir for the family bookshelf. We produce a structured, source-cited care passport with provenance, ACP topic coverage, and a professional-facing query layer. They have no case-manager workflow, no clinical schema, no DPIA / NEN 7510 story, no Dutch/EU hosting commitment. The capture UX is a commodity; the retrieval/evidence layer is the moat.
- **Risk:** They could pivot toward eldercare retrieval. Pre-empt by anchoring on case-manager workflow + Dutch reimbursement.

### Storii — https://www.storii.com/
- **What:** Phone-call (landline OK) life-story interviewer, weekly questions, AI biography rewrites, paid annual subscription.
- **Geography / stage:** UK/US, early-commercial; explicit dementia/reminiscence positioning ([AgeInPlaceTech](https://www.ageinplacetech.com/pressrelease/storii-creates-persons-life-memoir-over-phone)).
- **Overlap (axis 1):** High on capture, medium on dementia framing. Sister product StoriiCare is a care-home activities platform, not a clinical preference store.
- **Why we still win:** Same gap — no structured ACP/preferences schema, no integration with Dutch case-management workflow, no source-cited Q&A for HCPs. They optimise for reminiscence (good feeling), we optimise for evidence-backed retrieval (good decisions).

### StoryCorps Memory Loss Initiative — https://storycorps.org/discover/memory-loss-initiative/
- **What:** Non-profit oral-history program; 1,800+ recorded interviews archived at the Library of Congress; "Commemorate" toolkit for organisations.
- **Geography / stage:** US non-profit, mature, philanthropic.
- **Overlap (axis 1):** Conceptual overlap (preserving voice in dementia) but not productised for care workflows.
- **Why we still win:** They are an archive, not a tool. Useful as evidence the *idea* of preserving the patient voice has academic and cultural legitimacy ([Savundranayagam et al. 2011, *Dementia*](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1471301211408123)). Cite as validation, not as a rival.

### Tell Mel, Life Story AI, StoriedLife AI, HereAfter, Memory Lane, Eternos
- **What:** AI phone/voice biographers ("interactive memory app", "voice clone after death"). Eternos raised $10.3M in Nov 2025 ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/11/11/immortality-startup-eternos-pivots-to-a-personal-ai-that-sounds-like-you/)).
- **Overlap (axis 1):** Crowded consumer voice-memoir layer.
- **Why we still win:** None target healthcare buyers, ACP, or case managers; their wedge is gifting/legacy. The category proves capture works at scale; nobody has built the professional retrieval surface.

## B. Advance Care Planning (ACP) tools

### Praat op Tijd / Leidraad Proactieve Zorgplanning — Palliaweb / Patiëntenfederatie
- **What:** Dutch national ACP guidance + a newly-developed digital fillable form for uniform documentation of treatment wishes, co-developed by NHG, Verenso, NVAVG, V&VN, Patiëntenfederatie ([Palliaweb leidraad](https://palliaweb.nl/proactieve-zorgplanning), [zorgwensenvoorlater.nu PDF](https://zorgwensenvoorlater.nu/storage/Leidraad-proactieve-zorgplanning.pdf)). "Praat op Tijd" itself is Koraal's ACP toolkit for people with intellectual disabilities ([Koraal](https://www.koraal.nl/gehandicaptenzorg/wat-maakt-ons-bijzonder/zorg-dichtbij/advance-care-planning)).
- **Overlap (axis 2):** High — same regulator/professional ecosystem we must integrate with.
- **Why we still win / where we don't:** This is the *standard*, not a competitor — it's a form/leidraad, not a capture tool. We should explicitly export to its schema. Risk: if VWS/Patiëntenfederatie later commission a national ACP capture app, that becomes our reference platform. Build to plug in, not displace.

### MyDirectives — https://www.mydirectives.com/
- **What:** US digital advance-directive platform, HITRUST-certified, cloud-stored uADD with provider exchange.
- **Geography / stage:** US, mature, integrates with EHRs.
- **Overlap (axis 2):** Medium — outputs are legal directives; ours are not.
- **Why we still win:** They assume the user can fill structured forms today. Our wedge is *before* form-filling capacity exists, capturing values/routines/fears in the patient's own words. Dutch wilsverklaring is also a legal artefact KNMG warns shouldn't be auto-generated ([KNMG](https://www.knmg.nl/actueel/dossiers/levenseinde-2/zelfbeschikking/wilsverklaring)) — we should not produce legally binding output.

### Cake (now "Altogether") — https://www.joincake.com/
- **What:** Consumer end-of-life planning platform; wills, advance directives, funeral. Acquired by Foundation Partners Group in 2024.
- **Overlap (axis 2):** Low for our wedge — they target healthy adults planning ahead, sold via funeral-home channel.
- **Why we still win:** Different buyer (consumer/funeral vs. case manager); different artefact (legal docs vs. searchable voice evidence).

### ACP Decisions — https://www.acpdecisions.org/
- **What:** Dr. Angelo Volandes' video-decision-aid library, 500+ videos, 25 languages, RCT-validated for nursing-home dementia ACP ([JAMA Network 2018](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29868778/)).
- **Overlap (axis 2):** Adjacent — they educate the patient *into* an ACP conversation; we capture what comes out of one (and what comes out of life in general).
- **Why we still win:** Complementary, not competing. Pitch as "ACP Decisions makes the conversation possible; we make the patient's voice durable across years."

## C. Care passport / dementia preference platforms

### "This is me" — Alzheimer's Society UK — https://www.alzheimers.org.uk/get-support/publications-factsheets/this-is-me
- **What:** Free paper leaflet (4th edition), RCN-endorsed since 2010, captures who-the-person-is for hospital/care-home staff.
- **Overlap (axis 3):** Highest conceptual overlap of any product. This is our category-defining incumbent.
- **Why we still win:** It is paper, one-shot, completed by family/staff (not the patient), and not searchable. Evidence shows uptake and fidelity in hospitals are mixed ([Baillie & Thomas 2020, *Dementia*](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/1471301218778907); [PubMed integrative review](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33555640/)). We are "This is Me, voice-native, longitudinal, queryable, with provenance." Pitch frame: we should *literally name-check* This is Me as the inspiration and position as the digital, patient-authored evolution.

### NHS Health & Care Passport / MiCode QR passport
- **What:** NHS England plain-English health-and-care passport ([NHS England](https://www.england.nhs.uk/long-read/health-and-care-passport-plain-english/)); MiCode QR-code passport piloting in UK dementia.
- **Overlap (axis 3):** Medium — UK-only, form-driven, no voice capture.
- **Why we still win:** Different country, different artefact. Useful precedent that "passport" framing lands with health systems.

### Herinneringen / levensboek tradition (NL)
- **What:** Dutch "levensboek" practice, supported by Humanitas volunteers, Vilans/Zorginzicht templates, Innovatiekring Dementie ([IDé](https://www.innovatiekringdementie.nl/a-1931/levensverhalen-en-dierbare-herinneringen-vastleggen/)).
- **Overlap (axis 3):** Cultural — every Dutch case manager knows what a levensboek is.
- **Why we still win:** Levensboek is paper/photo, family-driven, not searchable, not exported to ECD. Pitch: "digital, voice-first levensboek with care-passport export."

## D. Dutch case-management / ECD systems

### Nedap Ons — https://nedap-ons.nl/
- **What:** Dominant Dutch ECD for V&V/GHZ/GGZ. 1,900+ institutions, 400k+ professionals; 67 of 115 examined elderly-care institutions use it ([Consultancy.nl](https://www.consultancy.nl/nieuws/55950/nedap-de-grootste-ecd-leverancier-binnen-ouderenzorg-en-gehandicaptenzorg)). Has Caren as the family-facing portal ([caren.nl](https://caren.nl/)).
- **Overlap (axis 4 + 7):** They own the system of record. They could ship a voice-capture module — but their roadmap signals are capacity planning and Luna (long-stay-at-home), not preference capture.
- **Why we still win:** Building an ECD is not our game. Build *to* Ons via export/integration; sell to providers as "the missing pre-documentation layer that fills Caren and Ons with the patient's actual words." Caren is the obvious distribution surface for the patient/family side.

### PinkRoccade Healthcare — mijnCaress — https://www.pinkroccade-healthcare.nl/
- **What:** #2 Dutch ECD for V&V/home-care/GHZ. Same logic as Nedap.
- **Overlap (axis 4):** Same — integration target, not a competitor unless they ship voice capture.

### Cordaan / Amstelring / Buurtzorg in-house tools
- **Overlap:** Low. These are buyers, not builders. Worth confirming that none of the 65 regional dementia networks ([DNN](https://dementienetwerknederland.nl/regionale-netwerken/)) has commissioned a voice-capture pilot.

## E. AI companions / monitoring for older adults

### ElliQ (Intuition Robotics) — https://elliq.com/
- **What:** Tabletop AI companion robot; daily check-ins, wellness, video calls, reminders. NY State Office for the Aging deployed ~800 units, reported 95% loneliness reduction ([NY OFA](https://aging.ny.gov/elliq-proactive-care-companion-initiative)). 2025 launch of ElliQ Caregiver Solution ([Robot Report](https://www.therobotreport.com/elliq-caregiver-solution-intuition-robotics-remote-home-care/)).
- **Overlap (axis 5):** Medium — they capture conversations, target older adults, expanding to caregivers.
- **Why we still win:** ElliQ is a $1500 device + subscription, English-only, not Dutch-deployed, optimised for loneliness/engagement not preference extraction. We are software-first on a channel (WhatsApp) seniors already use. They could add a "care passport" feature — possible but unlikely first-order roadmap; they're chasing Medicare/state wellness contracts, not Dutch case management.

### Sensi.AI — https://www.sensi.ai/
- **What:** Audio-based "Care Intelligence" — passive ambient listening in the home, detects falls/distress/early dementia signals. $45M Series C Oct 2025 ([Calcalist](https://www.calcalistech.com/ctechnews/article/hyawomspex)). Used by 80% of major US home-care networks.
- **Overlap (axis 5):** Lower than it looks — they are *passive* audio surveillance for safety, we are *consented active* voice capture for preference documentation. Different consent model, different output, different buyer pain.
- **Why we still win:** Sensi is a clinical-signals product (analogous to remote patient monitoring); we are a documentation-and-retrieval product. They could conceivably extend to "what does she usually say about food?" but it's not their pitch and the always-on mic privacy posture is much harder in NL/EU under AVG/AI Act.

### Replika-for-elders, Embodied (Moxie shutdown 2024), Inspiren
- **Overlap:** Mostly companionship/clinical-monitoring. Not directly competing for the case-manager workflow.

## F. Caregiver-coordination apps

### Familienet (NL) — https://www.familienet.nl/
- **What:** Family/care-team messaging app for Dutch elderly-care facilities; photos, updates, "Meehelp" tasks.
- **Overlap (axis 6):** Low on preference capture, high on social-distribution model.
- **Why we still win:** They are a comms layer, not a knowledge layer. Possible distribution partner for family-facing onboarding.

### Caren (Nedap) — https://caren.nl/
- **What:** Family/client portal tied to Nedap Ons; family can view/contribute to the care file.
- **Overlap (axes 6 + 7):** Medium. The natural place for our care passport to surface to the family.
- **Why we still win:** Caren shows formal care-record content to family; it does not capture the patient's spoken voice over time. Integration target.

### CaringBridge / Lotsa Helping Hands / Carely
- **Overlap:** US/coordination-focused; not players in the Dutch case-management workflow.

## G. PGOs / health-record adjacents (NL)

### Quli, Ivido, other MedMij-certified PGOs
- **What:** Dutch personal health environments aggregating data from GP/hospital/pharmacy via MedMij ([Rijksoverheid PGO](https://www.rijksoverheid.nl/onderwerpen/digitale-gegevens-in-de-zorg/vraag-en-antwoord/waar-kan-ik-een-persoonlijke-gezondheidsomgeving-pgo-voor-gebruiken)).
- **Overlap (axis 7):** Adjacent. PGOs are records of clinical *facts*, not patient *voice*. Quli notes caregivers can act on behalf of someone with dementia.
- **Why we still win:** PGOs do not capture preferences/values; they aggregate forms. Eventually we may export to a PGO, but the wedge is the case-manager dashboard, not consumer PGO adoption (which has been famously slow).

## H. Generalist AI-memory plays

### Limitless (acquired by Meta, Dec 2025), Personal.ai, Rewind (sunset)
- **What:** Lifelogging, ambient transcription. Limitless's pendant-and-software lifelog was bought by Meta and hardware sales killed in Dec 2025 ([WinBuzzer](https://winbuzzer.com/2025/12/05/meta-acquires-ai-wearables-startup-limitless-kills-pendant-sales-and-sunsets-rewind-app-xcxwbn/)).
- **Overlap (axis 8):** Low for our wedge. Different buyer, different consent model, no clinical positioning.
- **Why we still win:** Generalist memory plays are pivoting into Meta-scale platform features. They will not own the dementia case-manager workflow — but if Meta ships "WhatsApp Memory" inside WhatsApp itself, that changes the channel story. Worth tracking.

## I. Big-tech / platforms

### Apple Health/Journal, Google Health voice memos, Microsoft Healthcare Bot
- **Overlap:** Latent. None has shipped a "patient voice for case managers" product. Apple Journal added voice notes; Google has discontinued multiple consumer-health bets; Microsoft Healthcare Bot is task automation.
- **Amazon Alexa Together** was discontinued in 2024 and replaced with the narrower "Alexa Emergency Assist" in May 2025 ([SafeWise](https://www.safewise.com/what-is-alexa-together/)). Signals that big-tech caregiver pivots have not been profitable consumer plays.
- **Why we still win:** Big tech is a channel risk (Meta owns WhatsApp), not a product competitor today. Mitigation: design for channel-agnostic capture (WhatsApp + phone-call fallback + assisted in-person).

## J. Academic / public-sector NL

- **Vilans** — runs "DEMTECH" and tech-at-home dementia work, surveys mantelzorgers using AI chatbots ([Vilans](https://www.vilans.nl/actueel/verhalen/ai-chatbots-zelfredzaamheid-zorg)). Knowledge institute, not a vendor — possible research/validation partner.
- **Alzheimercentrum Amsterdam / Amsterdam UMC** — TAP-dementia diagnosis project, no voice-capture product.
- **Ben Sajet Centrum, HvA, VU, UNO Amsterdam** — research/ethics partners, not competitors.
- **Risk:** A ZonMw-funded consortium could commission something similar. Get in early as a research partner, not a target.

## Strategic takeaways

1. **The capture layer is crowded; the retrieval-for-professionals layer is empty.** A dozen consumer AI biographers (Memoirji, Storii, Tell Mel, HereAfter, Eternos) already do voice-note life-story capture. Nobody owns the dementia case-manager dashboard with source-cited Q&A and ACP-schema export. That is our wedge — not "we built a WhatsApp voice bot."
2. **Position as the digital, voice-native evolution of "This is Me" and the Dutch levensboek — not a new category.** Both have professional credibility (RCN endorsement; Vilans/Zorginzicht templates; Alzheimer Nederland reach) and known limitations (paper, one-shot, not searchable, not patient-authored). Anchoring there is more defensible than "AI memory for elders."
3. **ACP tools assume capacity at the moment of use; we capture *before* capacity declines.** MyDirectives, Cake, and the Dutch Leidraad all need a coherent author today. Our pitch is the upstream feeder: accumulate the patient's own voice on routines/values/fears so that when the formal ACP conversation happens, the case manager arrives with evidence, not a blank form.
4. **The ECD vendors (Nedap Ons, PinkRoccade) are integration targets, not competitors — but only if we move first.** Their roadmaps are capacity, billing, and Caren-style family portals. A voice-capture module is plausible-but-not-imminent. Build the export/integration story now (Ons API, Caren surfacing) so we are the obvious bolt-on before they build it.
5. **Privacy/consent is the moat against ambient-audio plays (Sensi.AI) and big-tech channel plays (Meta/WhatsApp).** Active consented capture, NEN 7510-aligned controls, DPIA done, EU hosting, source-cited (no hallucinated synthesis), human-in-the-loop sign-off — these are explicitly things Sensi's always-on-mic model and a hypothetical "WhatsApp Memory" feature cannot easily promise under AVG/AI Act / EHDS. Make this part of the pitch, not the appendix.

## Follow-up questions to investigate next

1. **Does Nedap Ons (and PinkRoccade mijnCaress) have a documented external API or app-marketplace for a third-party voice-capture module to plug into the case-manager view and Caren?** Would determine whether our "integration not displacement" story is technically real in 8 weeks.
2. **Has Patiëntenfederatie / NHG / Verenso commissioned (or signalled intent to commission) a national digital ACP capture tool around the new Leidraad?** If yes, our path is "reference implementation / capture front-end for the Leidraad" rather than parallel product.
3. **What is the formal data-processor / EU-hosting status of WhatsApp Business Cloud API for Dutch health data in 2026?** Currently the lowest-friction capture channel; if AP/Zorginstituut have published anything restrictive, we need a phone-call fallback as primary, not secondary.

## Things we should NOT claim in the pitch

- "First product to preserve the patient's voice in dementia." StoryCorps Memory Loss Initiative (since 2010) and 1,800+ Library-of-Congress recordings predate us.
- "First WhatsApp life-story bot." Memoirji is shipping now and is positioned as the leading WhatsApp memoir tool.
- "First AI care companion for elders." ElliQ is deployed at state scale; Sensi is in 80% of major US home-care networks; the category is funded.
- "Replaces the levensboek / This is Me / wilsverklaring / case manager." We extend, structure, and digitise — we do not replace any of these. KNMG, Rijksoverheid, and the Leidraad are explicit about boundaries.
- "Detects dementia / cognitive decline from voice." That is medical-device territory under MDR + AI Act. Sensi.AI walks that line; we should not.
- "Reimbursed by Zvw today." Reimbursement is a credible 12-24 month path via wijkverpleging/casemanagement, not a current claim.
